# Risk Management Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes the company’s approach to identifying, assessing, prioritizing, and treating cybersecurity and operational risks. The goal is to reduce the likelihood and impact of events that could harm the organization, its clients, or its data.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:

- All business units and departments  
- All information systems, cloud services, and technology assets  
- All employees, contractors, and third-party partners  

## 3. Risk Management Objectives
- Protect company and client data  
- Maintain confidentiality, integrity, and availability  
- Reduce exposure to security threats and business disruptions  
- Support informed decision-making through structured risk assessment  

## 4. Risk Management Process

### 4.1 Risk Identification
The following must be documented:
- Security threats (internal and external)  
- Technology weaknesses and misconfigurations  
- Operational and human-related risks  
- Third-party/vendor risks  

Sources may include audits, logs, user reports, vulnerability scans, and incident findings.

### 4.2 Risk Assessment
Each identified risk must be evaluated using:

- **Likelihood** (Low, Medium, High)  
- **Impact** (Low, Medium, High)  
- **Overall Risk Score** (Likelihood × Impact)  

Assessments must be documented in the company Risk Register.

### 4.3 Risk Treatment
Risks must be addressed using one of the following strategies:

- **Mitigate** – Implement controls to reduce risk  
- **Accept** – Acknowledge and monitor the risk  
- **Transfer** – Use insurance or third-party services  
- **Avoid** – Discontinue risky processes or systems  

### 4.4 Risk Monitoring
- Risks must be reviewed quarterly.  
- High-risk items must be reviewed monthly.  
- Status updates must be recorded in the Risk Register.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### Security Lead
- Owns the risk management program  
- Conducts assessments and recommends treatments  
- Ensures high-risk items are escalated to leadership  

### IT Administrator
- Implements technical controls for risk mitigation  
- Provides evidence and system information for assessments  

### Compliance Manager
- Ensures documentation meets regulatory and client requirements  
- Coordinates audits and external assessments  

### Department Managers
- Identify operational risks within their teams  
- Ensure controls are followed  

## 6. Risk Register Requirements
The Risk Register must include at minimum:

- Risk description  
- Root cause  
- Likelihood  
- Impact  
- Risk score  
- Assigned owner  
- Current status  
- Mitigation plan  
- Evidence or notes  

## 7. Reporting
- High-risk items must be reported to leadership immediately.  
- Monthly summaries must be reviewed by the Security Lead.  
- Significant changes in risk must be documented.

## 8. Policy Review
This policy must be reviewed annually or after major organizational or technology changes.
