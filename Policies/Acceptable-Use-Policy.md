# Acceptable Use Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes rules for acceptable use of company systems, data, cloud services, and equipment. The goal is to ensure responsible, secure, and lawful use of all technology resources.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:

- All employees  
- Contractors  
- Interns  
- Third-party users  
- All company-owned devices, networks, cloud applications, and communication systems  

## 3. Acceptable Use Requirements

### 3.1 General Use
- Use company resources primarily for business activities.  
- Limited personal use is allowed if it does not interfere with work or violate policy.  

### 3.2 Security Requirements
- Protect account credentials; do not share passwords.  
- Lock screens when away from a device.  
- Report suspicious activity immediately to the Security Lead.

### 3.3 Data Handling
- Access only the data required for job responsibilities.  
- Store confidential data only in approved systems (Google Workspace, company apps).  
- Do not email sensitive data to personal accounts.

### 3.4 Prohibited Actions
- Installing unauthorized software.  
- Bypassing security controls or attempting to hack systems.  
- Viewing, downloading, or distributing inappropriate or illegal content.  
- Using company devices for cryptocurrency mining, illegal downloads, or malicious activities.

## 4. Monitoring
The company may monitor system usage, logs, email, and network traffic to ensure compliance and investigate security incidents.

## 5. Violations
Failure to follow this policy may result in:

- Loss of access privileges  
- Disciplinary action  
- Termination for severe violations  

## 6. Responsibilities
### IT Administrator
- Enforces technical controls (device monitoring, logging, network security).

### Security Lead
- Investigates violations and provides user security training.

### Department Managers
- Ensures team members comply with acceptable use requirements.
