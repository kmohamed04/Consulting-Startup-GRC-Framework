# Incident Response Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy defines how the company identifies, reports, investigates, and responds to security incidents. The goal is to reduce business impact, restore operations quickly, and prevent recurrence.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, and interns  
- All company systems, cloud services, and data  
- Any security-related event affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability  

## 3. Definition of a Security Incident
A security incident includes, but is not limited to:

- Unauthorized access to systems or data  
- Lost or stolen devices  
- Malware infections or phishing compromises  
- System outages caused by malicious activity  
- Data leakage or accidental disclosure  
- Suspicious login attempts or account takeover  

## 4. Incident Response Lifecycle

### 4.1 Identification
- Employees must immediately report suspected incidents to the Security Lead.  
- Automated monitoring tools (Google Admin, endpoint logs) must be reviewed regularly.

### 4.2 Containment
- IT Administrator isolates impacted accounts, devices, or systems.  
- Network access or credentials may be temporarily disabled.

### 4.3 Eradication
- Identify root cause (malware, misconfiguration, stolen credentials).  
- Remove malicious files, disable compromised accounts, apply patches.  
- Strengthen controls to prevent recurrence.

### 4.4 Recovery
- Restore services and validate that systems are secure.  
- Monitor affected assets for abnormal behavior.

### 4.5 Lessons Learned
- The Security Lead documents the incident within 5 business days.  
- Findings are reviewed with management to improve processes and controls.

## 5. Reporting Requirements
Employees must immediately report:

- Phishing emails  
- Suspicious logins  
- Unauthorized access  
- Lost or stolen devices  
- Any activity that “feels wrong”  

Reports can be submitted through:
- Email to security@startup.com  
- Direct message to the Security Lead  
- Internal ticketing system  

## 6. Responsibilities

### Security Lead
- Directs incident response efforts  
- Conducts investigations  
- Documents incident reports  
- Coordinates communication with leadership  

### IT Administrator
- Executes containment and recovery steps  
- Restores affected systems  
- Performs technical evidence collection  

### Compliance Manager
- Ensures required regulatory notifications are completed (if applicable)  
- Tracks remediation and policy updates  

### Employees
- Report incidents immediately  
- Preserve evidence by not modifying affected systems  

## 7. Evidence Handling
- Logs, screenshots, emails, and system data must be preserved securely.  
- Only authorized staff may collect or view incident evidence.

## 8. Communication
- Sensitive details must not be shared outside the response team.  
- Public or customer notifications must be approved by leadership.

## 9. Enforcement
Failure to follow this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. Malicious actions may result in legal consequences.
