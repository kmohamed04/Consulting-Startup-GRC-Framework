# Governance Charter

## 1. Purpose
This charter defines how the company manages cybersecurity, compliance, and risk. It establishes the decision-making structure, responsibilities, and oversight needed to protect company systems, data, and assets.

## 2. Governance Objectives
- Maintain a clear security and compliance structure.
- Assign ownership for policies, controls, and risk.
- Ensure leadership oversight over cybersecurity decisions.
- Support alignment with standards such as NIST CSF and CIS Controls.
- Promote accountability and transparency.

## 3. Governance Structure
### Executive Leadership
- Provides strategic direction.
- Approves major security initiatives and budgets.

### Security Lead
- Operates the security program.
- Ensures compliance with frameworks.
- Reports risk and security gaps to leadership.

### IT Administrator
- Implements technical controls.
- Manages systems, configurations, and access.

### Compliance Manager (if applicable)
- Oversees audits, documentation, and evidence collection.
- Supports regulatory or customer-driven compliance needs.

## 4. Governance Activities
- Review risks quarterly.
- Approve policies and major updates.
- Monitor security projects and deadlines.
- Track compliance using the Compliance Checklist.
- Review audit results and remediation plans.

## 5. Approval
This charter is approved and enforced by company leadership.
